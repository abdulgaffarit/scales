About USA Salaries

Comprehensive salary intelligence powered by official government data.

USA Salaries provides accurate, up-to-date salary information for hundreds of occupations across every U.S. state and major city. Our mission is to help workers, job seekers, employers, and researchers make informed decisions about compensation.

What We Do

We transform complex government wage data into clear, accessible salary insights. Every data point on this site comes directly from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OES) program — the gold standard for occupational wage data in the United States.

📊 By the Numbers

  • 295+ occupations with detailed salary breakdowns
  • 51 geographic areas (all 50 states + District of Columbia)
  • Percentile data from entry-level (10th) to top earners (90th)
  • Year-over-year trends showing salary growth trajectories
  • Updated annually with each new BLS data release

Who This Is For

  • Job seekers — Research fair compensation before interviews and negotiations
  • Employees — Benchmark your current salary against national and state averages
  • Employers & HR — Set competitive pay ranges based on real market data
  • Students — Explore career paths and their earning potential
  • Researchers — Access structured wage data for analysis

Our Data Source

All salary figures come from the BLS Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OES) survey, which collects data from approximately 1.1 million business establishments across the United States. The OES program produces employment and wage estimates for over 800 detailed occupations.

The current data reflects the May 2025 OES release, published by the Bureau of Labor Statistics. This represents the most recent comprehensive occupational wage data available for the United States.

How We're Different

Unlike salary sites that rely on self-reported data (which can be biased and unreliable), USA Salaries uses exclusively official government statistics. This means our figures represent actual employer-reported wages — not estimates, not crowdsourced guesses, but real compensation data collected through a rigorous federal survey program.
